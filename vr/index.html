Suspended Assembly

VR Project · Drum Interaction

A VR rhythm-drumming build made in Unity. The left hand grips the drum, the right hand swings a stick, and strike speed drives volume. Two clips below show two different interaction approaches; the full Unity project is too large to host here, but the core logic is summarized underneath.
Clip 1

Live Hand-Tracked Interaction

Each hand uses a different interaction model. The left hand detects trigger and grab input. The right hand tracks strike velocity — at the moment of impact, it looks back over the previous 10 frames and takes the highest velocity value; if it exceeds a set threshold, a synthesized accent hit plays.

Clip 2

Scripted Animation, Real-Time Audio

A button press triggers a pre-built animation, but the stick-to-drumhead contact sound is synthesized live. Striking within 0.4s of entering the "heavy zone" plays an accent hit; otherwise it plays a light hit. The left-hand trigger simulates a braking/stop sound.
